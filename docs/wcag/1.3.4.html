WCAG 2.2 · Level AA

1.3.4 Orientation

Success Criterion

Content does not restrict its view and operation to a single display orientation, such as portrait or landscape, unless a specific display orientation is essential. Note Examples where a particular display orientation may be essential are a bank check, a piano application, slides for a projector or television, or virtual reality content where content is not necessarily restricted to landscape or portrait display orientation.

Understanding 1.3.4 on W3C

Sufficient techniques

  • G214 Using a control to allow access to content in different orientations which is otherwise restricted

Documented failures anti-patterns

  • F97 Failure due to locking the orientation to landscape or portrait view
  • F100 Failure of Success Criterion 1.3.4 due to showing a message asking to reorient device

ACT rules covering this SC mechanical

  • b33eff Orientation of the page is not restricted using CSS transforms
